Comparative Programming Languages is a module designed to introduce students to the principles and practical differences among major programming paradigms and language families. The module explores how language design influences problem-solving, software structure, and implementation style, while helping students develop a broader understanding of programming beyond any single language environment.

As a tutor for this module at Dublin City University, responsibilities included supporting class sessions, clarifying core concepts, assisting students with exercises and assignments, and grading project work. The teaching support focused on helping students compare language features, understand syntax and semantics, and apply programming concepts effectively across different languages and paradigms.

The module also aimed to strengthen students’ analytical and technical confidence, enabling them to evaluate languages in terms of readability, abstraction, efficiency, and suitability for different computational tasks. Through class support and project feedback, the role contributed to student learning, assessment, and development of practical programming skills.
